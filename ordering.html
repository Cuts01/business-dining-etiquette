Ordering & Drinks

Build a low-stress order

Pick “conversation-friendly” food

Choose dishes you can cut with a fork and knife and eat in small, tidy bites. Think grilled chicken, fish, or pasta instead of ribs, giant burgers, or messy tacos.

Stay in the middle of the menu

Aim for mid-priced options rather than the cheapest or most expensive item. It shows respect for the host and the company budget.

Match the host’s rhythm

Order roughly the same number of courses as the host so you are eating at the same pace. Avoid adding extra courses that will delay the group.

Ask questions briefly

Clarify allergies or preferences in one or two simple questions, then decide. Long negotiations with the server slow down the meal.

Simple rule: if you would need both hands, a bib, or a pile of napkins, choose something else.

Deciding about drinks

You never have to drink alcohol to be polite. Choose the option that keeps you the most focused and comfortable.

Skip alcohol

Sparkling water, soda, or a mocktail keep your attention fully on the conversation.

One drink

If you drink, nurse a single beer, wine, or simple cocktail through the meal.

Stop early

If others continue ordering rounds, switch to water or coffee without making a big announcement.

Conversation tip

Use the time after ordering to turn the focus to the purpose of the meeting. Invite others in with open questions instead of jumping straight into a pitch.
