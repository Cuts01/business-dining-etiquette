Do's & Don'ts

Before the meal

Do

Confirm date, time, location, and dress code with the host or organizer.

Arrive 5–10 minutes early and bring a short list of topics you want to cover.

Don't

Schedule another meeting immediately after the meal so that you have to rush out.

Show up very hungry, flustered, or talking only about how busy and stressed you are.

At the table

Do

Place your napkin on your lap after the host does and use utensils from the outside in.

Keep your phone out of sight and help draw quieter people into the conversation.

Don't

Drop keys, bags, or laptops on the table; they make the setting look cluttered.

Interrupt, talk over others, or answer messages while someone is speaking.

Ordering & drinks

Do

Choose mid-priced dishes that are easy to eat in small bites.

If you drink, match the host’s pace and stay within your professional limits.

Don't

Order the most expensive item or foods that splatter, drip, or require lots of hands-on eating.

Pressure others to drink or comment on how much anyone is drinking.

Ending the meal

Do

Thank the host, recap one or two outcomes, and confirm any follow-up actions or dates.

Send a short, specific thank-you message the same day or the next morning.

Don't

Leave without thanking people individually when possible.

Bring up new complex topics just as the bill arrives or everyone needs to leave.
